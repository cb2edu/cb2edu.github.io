CB2-201: Computational Biology and Bioinformatics (GBSC 703-01E)

We are happy to announce 2016 edition of the course “CB2-201: Computational Biology and Bioinformatics”, offered under the umbrella of CB2 initiative at UAB (http://www.uab.edu/cb2). The course will start on February 15 and will run for two consecutive weeks. The course is a hands-on, rigorous, and fast-paced introduction to essential skills in modern Bioinformatics and Computational Biology, modeled after the NSF "immersive learning" proposal (http://www.nsf.gov/pubs/2008/nsf08204/index.jsp).





  • Date and Time
    Feb 15 – 26, 2016. 9:30 AM to 1:30 PM everyday.

  • Location
    Volker Hall VH-G073

  • Syllabus
    1. Technical aspects and high-performance computing (HPC) 101 - talk
    2. Biological Data Sources and File Formats - handout and talf
    3. Sequence Alignment
    4. Homology search – BLAST, HMMER, PSSMs talk and handout
    5. Evolutionary and comparative genomics; molecular evolution - handout and talk
    6. Protein Structure; mutational effects on structure; varianteffect predictors.
    7. NGS: NGS data quality assessment and preprocessing - handout and talk
    8. NGS: Alignment to reference; BAM; SAM.
    9. NGS: Variant Calling, VCF, Tabix
    10. NGS: Introduction to RNAseq analysis; RSEM - handout
      11. and talk
